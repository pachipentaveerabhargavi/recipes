Pootha Rekulu

About !

Pootharekulu, also known as Pootha Rekulu, is a popular sweet from the state of Andhra Pradesh in India. It’s a delicate and unique sweet made with rice flour, ghee, and sugar

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup cashews, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup almonds, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup cardamom powder
  • Edible silver leaves (varak), optional for garnish

how to make it?

Preparing the Rice Flour Sheets:

  • Prepare a thin rice flour batter by mixing rice flour with water. The consistency should be similar to that of dosa batter.
  • Heat a non-stick pan and grease it lightly with ghee.
  • Pour a ladleful of the rice flour batter and quickly spread it into a thin sheet using circular motions. Cook until it forms a thin, translucent sheet.
  • Remove the sheet carefully and let it cool.
  • Repeat this process to make more rice flour sheets. You’ll need around 15-20 sheets.

Preparing the Pootharekulu:

  • Take one rice flour sheet and apply a thin layer of ghee over it.
  • Sprinkle powdered sugar, cashews, almonds, and cardamom powder evenly over the sheet.
  • Carefully roll the sheet from one end to form a cylindrical shape.
  • Repeat this process for all the sheets.
  • Once done, trim the edges of the rolls
  • Garnish the rolls with edible silver leaves (varak), if using.
