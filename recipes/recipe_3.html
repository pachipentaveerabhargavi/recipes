Chepa Pulusu

About!

Chepala pulusu is an Andhra fish curry, which is a famous spicy seafood recipe, prepared by cooking fresh fish pieces in tangy tamarind sauce with lots of hot spices.

Ingredients

    To marinate:

  • 1 kg fish
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ lemon

    • To dry roast & grind:

  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds
  • 1 tsp peppercorns
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp fenugreek seeds

    • Other ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp gingelly oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • ½ tsp fenugreek
  • 12 shallots
  • 3 green chillies
  • 2 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • ½ tbsp chilli powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ¼ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 tsp crystal salt
  • 2 cups tamarind water

how to make it?

Preparation:

  • Wash fish pieces thoroughly 2-3 times in lots of water.
  • Drain water completely and marinate with turmeric powder, salt, and lemon juice.
  • Lemon and salt tenderize the fish, while turmeric removes impurities.
  • Set aside the marination for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  • Peel and chop small onions (shallots).
  • Soak tamarind in warm water for 15 minutes, then extract 2 cups of thick tamarind water and discard the pulp.
  • Prepare ginger garlic paste using 8 garlic cloves and 2 inches of ginger if not available.
  • Rinse and chop tomatoes.

How to Make Pulusu Masala:

  • In a pan, dry roast peppercorns, cumin (jeera), and fenugreek (methi) seeds for 5 minutes until aromatic.
  • Transfer to a mixer and add poppy seeds (kuskus).
  • Grind into a fine paste using a small amount of water.
  • Set the pulusu masala paste aside.

How to Make Chepala Pulusu:

  • Heat a large kadai and add gingelly oil.
  • Add mustard seeds, fennel seeds, and fenugreek seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add chopped shallots, green chillies, and curry leaves. Sauté until onions turn light brown.
  • Add ginger garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell goes away.
  • Add chopped tomato and cook until soft.
  • Add spice powders (turmeric and chilli powder).
  • Pour tamarind water, mix, cover, and cook for 5 minutes to remove the raw smell.
  • Add the pulusu masala paste and mix well.
  • Boil the gravy for 5 minutes and add required salt.
  • Gently drop in the marinated fish pieces one by one into the gravy.
  • Stir lightly with a wooden ladle to make space. Avoid vigorous stirring to prevent fish from breaking.
  • Cover and cook on low flame for no more than 5 minutes.
  • Switch off the flame once the fish meat turns white.
  • Let it rest for an hour before serving for enhanced taste.
  • Serve hot with rice.
